# PRODUCT.md — Game Review Magazine

## What this product is

**Name:** Game Review Magazine
**One-liner:** A dark editorial gaming publication with cinematic reviews, a 1–10 scoring system, and category-filtered news — built for serious gamers who want criticism, not hype.
**Core user action:** Read a game review and trust the score.

---

## User

**Primary audience:** Serious gamers aged 16–35
**Mindset:** Critical, informed, taste-driven. They've played hundreds of games. They don't need a tutorial. They need a publication that respects their intelligence.
**What they want from a review site:**
- A score they can trust, not inflated by ad relationships
- Writing that treats games as art and culture
- Fast access to reviews without UI noise getting in the way
- Editorial voice — not a spec sheet

**What they hate:**
- Ad clutter and pop-ups that interrupt reading
- Scores handed out without conviction (7.5 for everything)
- Sites that feel like press release aggregators
- Walls of unstructured community text (Reddit energy)

---

## Sections

| Section | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| **Reviews** | Scored editorial reviews. The core product. |
| **News** | Game announcements, trailers, industry events. Short, factual, fast. |

No guides. No walkthroughs. This is a magazine, not a wiki.

---

## Scoring system

**Scale:** 1–10 (whole numbers only — no 7.5s)
**Philosophy:** Scores mean something. A 10 is rare. A 5 is not an insult — it means average. The score should be defensible in the copy.

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 10 | Essential. Generation-defining. |
| 8–9 | Excellent. Buy it. |
| 6–7 | Good with caveats. |
| 4–5 | Average or flawed. |
| 1–3 | Poor. Avoid or for fans only. |

## References (what to emulate)

| Reference | What to take from it |
|-----------|---------------------|
| **Polygon** | Cinematic review pages, strong visual storytelling, full-bleed imagery, long-form quality |
| **The Verge (dark mode)** | Sharp editorial grid, bold typography system, clean information hierarchy, tech-forward restraint |

**Combined north star:** The visual density and type boldness of The Verge, applied to the cinematic atmosphere and review depth of Polygon. Dark by default, always.

---

## Anti-references (what to reject)

| Anti-reference | Why |
|----------------|-----|
| **Metacritic** | Purely utilitarian. No editorial voice. Aggregate UI with no personality. |
| **Reddit-style UX** | No visual hierarchy. Walls of user text. No curation. Feels chaotic. |

---

## Tone & register

- **Voice:** Critical and atmospheric. Not academic, not hype. Like a film critic who also finishes every game.
- **Register:** Intelligent but not pretentious. Opinionated but argued.
- **What it's not:** PR copy. Listicles. Hype. Filler.

---

## Core design principles

1. **Cinematic above all** — Every review page should feel like entering the game world. Full-bleed hero, dark backgrounds, typography that commands attention.
2. **The score is sacred** — The rating display is the single most important UI element on a review page. It must be unmissable and feel weighty.
3. **Speed for readers** — No loading spinners for content already on the page. Reviews load fast. Filters respond instantly.
4. **Dark first, always** — Never render a white background as a default. Light mode does not exist.
5. **Editorial hierarchy** — Not all content is equal. Featured reviews get full-bleed treatment. The grid enforces a clear visual pecking order.
6. **No noise** — No intrusive ads in the reading flow. No pop-up newsletter banners mid-article. Respect the reader.
